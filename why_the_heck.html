Why the heck am I even doing this?

  • August 29, 2023
  • By Rudraksh Kuchiya

Hey there, you! Yeah, you know who you are – the one deep in the midst of that confusing whirlwind called life. Buckle up because we're diving into the maze of thoughts that have been swirling around in my noggin. It's like a cosmic puzzle, with pieces from heartbreaks, dreams, and chasing that elusive thing called purpose.

Man, I've been stuck in this whirlwind of questions lately. Like, seriously, why am I hustling so damn hard for classes that I'll probably forget as soon as my exams are over? And what's the deal with me jamming on those drums and snapping pics? Don't even get me started on my drone fascination – like, who knew I'd be so into that stuff? Oh, and let's not forget the friend chats, the daily grind, and this dumb lab that's sucking the life out of me.

Speaking of matters of the heart, here's the twist: Why do I even like that girl? Why is there this burning desire for her to like me back? And let’s not forget the constant hunger for recognition and validation – it's like I'm chasing some mythical beast. And oh, the rat race! I'm supposed to get a high-paying job, earn heaps of money, and own shiny things like houses and cars. But honestly, why? Why put so much stock in all these things?

So, here's the scoop after a lot of head scratching: I thought maybe it's all about finding someone to share life with, you know? That one person you can cozy up with in your own little nest. But then again, is that really the ultimate goal? I mean, sure, having a partner can make your world go crazy in a good way, everything seems brighter and all. But is that all there is? Is everything we do, like getting a house, earning money, just to have someone to share it with?

So, I tried to figure it out, diving deep into my brain's nooks and crannies. Maybe it's about laughing and growing with my buddies, the real homies. Those folks who I can mess around with and be my weird self around without being judged. I get this rush when I'm with them, and it's like my life is on overdrive with them by my side. I see us all, years down the line, still tight like a bunch of peas in a pod. But then, who knows, life can throw curveballs.

So, I decided to embrace the "live in the moment" mantra. I chilled about the future, tried to make every dull day a standout memory. And it was a blast... for a bit. But then, man, I started feeling adrift. Like, what's the point of it all? I began to question my purpose, got tangled up in an existential crisis. But, believe it or not, I found a silver lining.

Turns out, deep down, I'm in this rollercoaster of life to feed that little fire that's been flickering since I was a tiny human. Remember those wild dreams that you never dared share because they sounded too crazy? Yeah, those. For me, it was the dream of soaring high, becoming a pilot. I was all in – researching, dreaming, visualizing. But then, bam! Life hit me with a curveball of its own – my vision wasn't pilot-material. It was like falling into a void.

But guess what? My superhero mom swooped in and became my safety net. I changed lanes, setting my sights on the next best thing: drones. And guess what? The spark was back – brighter than ever. Fast forward to today – I'm on the brink of launching ventures in the drone world. And you know what? It's crazy how far I've come without realizing it. But I'm not here to brag.

Yeah, sometimes I still get lost in the noise, questioning why I'm grinding so hard. That's why I'm spilling my thoughts onto this page – to remind myself that it's not about mourning lost love, not about chasing jobs that don't resonate, not about clinging to friends like a security blanket. It's about that little version of me, the one who dared to dream big.

Sure, people lose their way sometimes, forgetting why they started in the first place. They get caught up in the "shoulds" and let the "wants" fade into the background. But listen up, here's the real deal: Follow that inner monster of yours, that spark that's uniquely you. It could be anything – seriously, anything. The car, the job, the partner, they'll come when they need to. But the true journey is all about nurturing what makes your heart race, your soul dance, and your eyes light up.
