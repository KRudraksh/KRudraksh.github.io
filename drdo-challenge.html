DRDO - UAV Guided UGV Navigation Challenge

  • Mar 2022
  • By Rudraksh Kuchiya

Secured 3rd place nationally in the DRDO - UAV guided UGV challenge at Inter IIT Tech Meet 10.0, competing against 12 other renowned IITs. The project focused on UAV-assisted terrain scanning for UGV trajectory identification and tracking, implementing advanced control systems and trajectory planning algorithms.

Project Overview

The DRDO UAV Guided UGV Challenge focused on developing systems where unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) guide unmanned ground vehicles (UGVs) through complex terrain. Our team created a sophisticated system that used aerial surveillance to map terrain and guide ground vehicles along optimal paths, implementing advanced control algorithms for precise navigation and obstacle avoidance.

UGV navigating through complex terrain with UAV guidance

Technical Achievements

Control Systems

  • Deployed Stanley controller for precise steering commands to the UGV
  • Implemented PID controller to manage throttle inputs and maintain optimal speed
  • Integrated control systems with UAV feedback for real-time path corrections

Trajectory Generation

  • Utilized cubic and quintic spline interpolators to generate smooth trajectories from waypoints
  • Optimized path planning for minimal jerk and acceleration changes
  • Incorporated radius of curvature-based velocity adaptation for dynamic feasibility

UAV-UGV Integration

  • Established robust communication between aerial and ground vehicles using ROS
  • Implemented Ardupilot with MAVROS for reliable control and telemetry
  • Developed algorithms to eliminate disturbances in UGV tracking based on UAV feedback

Simulation of the UAV-UGV coordination system

Technical Approach

Path Planning and Trajectory Generation

Our approach to path planning involved several key components:

  • Initial waypoint generation based on UAV terrain scanning
  • Spline-based interpolation between waypoints to ensure smooth transitions
  • Optimization of trajectories to minimize control effort and maintain dynamic feasibility

The use of cubic and quintic spline interpolators was crucial for generating paths that the UGV could follow efficiently. These interpolators ensured continuity in position, velocity, and acceleration profiles, resulting in smoother vehicle motion and reduced mechanical stress.

Stanley Controller Implementation

The Stanley controller was selected for its robust performance in steering control for ground vehicles. Our implementation included:

  • Cross-track error minimization for accurate path following
  • Heading error compensation to maintain proper vehicle orientation
  • Adaptive gain tuning based on vehicle speed and path characteristics

Velocity Adaptation

To ensure safe and efficient navigation, we implemented a velocity adaptation system that:

  • Analyzed the path's radius of curvature in real-time
  • Adjusted vehicle speed to maintain stability during turns
  • Accelerated on straightaways and decelerated for sharp curves
  • Considered vehicle dynamics constraints to prevent skidding or tipping

UAV Feedback Integration

The UAV provided critical feedback for the UGV navigation system:

  • Real-time position updates to correct for UGV localization drift
  • Terrain analysis to identify potential obstacles or challenging sections
  • Path replanning capabilities when deviations or obstacles were detected

The integration of aerial and ground vehicles created a robust navigation system capable of adapting to complex environments and maintaining precise tracking even in challenging conditions.

Results and Impact

Our team's solution demonstrated exceptional performance in the competition:

  • Secured 3rd place nationally among 12 prestigious IITs
  • Achieved high accuracy in UGV path following with minimal deviations
  • Successfully demonstrated robust performance in various simulated environments

The technology developed for this challenge has potential applications in:

  • Military reconnaissance and supply missions
  • Disaster response and search operations
  • Agricultural automation and precision farming
  • Remote exploration of hazardous environments
