ProjectsiMECHE

Unmanned Aerial Systems 2023

  • Dec 2022 - June 2023
  • By Rudraksh Kuchiya

Led a successful team of nine engineers in the iMECHE UAS Challenge in London, designing and developing innovative UAVs for guided payload delivery in humanitarian missions. The team earned three prestigious awards: Best Design, Scrutineering Award for supportability, and the Advancement Award.

Project Overview

The iMECHE UAS Challenge focuses on designing unmanned aerial systems for humanitarian aid missions. Our team developed a 7 kg relief drone capable of lifting 6 kg of payload with a flight time exceeding 20 minutes. The drone was specifically engineered for precision delivery of humanitarian aid in challenging environments.

The drone in flight during field testing

Technical Achievements

Design Optimization

  • Reduced weight of design by 23.6% through topological optimization on carbon fiber plates in ANSYS
  • Designed a lightweight yet robust frame capable of withstanding operational stresses
  • Optimized propulsion system for maximum efficiency and flight time

Precision Control Systems

  • Achieved servo-operated payload drop in representative humanitarian aid mission with accuracy of 10 cm
  • Implemented advanced flight control algorithms for stability in various weather conditions
  • Developed custom control interfaces for mission planning and execution

Safety Features

  • Incorporated comprehensive safety systems including datalink loss protocols
  • Implemented geo-fencing to ensure operation within designated areas
  • Developed Ground Control Fail Safe system for secure flight termination when necessary

Smart Return-to-Launch System

  • Developed an intelligent RTL (Return to Launch) system based on analysis of over 50 flight logs
  • Created predictive algorithms for allowable flight time with 90% accuracy
  • Implemented battery monitoring systems with real-time performance analysis

Competition Results

Our team's exceptional performance at the iMECHE UAS Challenge in Buckminster, United Kingdom resulted in three prestigious awards:

  • Best Design Award - Recognizing the innovative engineering solutions and optimized design of our UAV
  • Scrutineering Award for Supportability - Highlighting the maintainability and operational efficiency of our system
  • Advancement Award - Acknowledging our contributions to advancing UAV technology for humanitarian applications

The team demonstrated exceptional engineering capabilities, creating a drone that not only met but exceeded the competition requirements for humanitarian aid delivery.

Impact and Applications

The drone system we developed has potential applications beyond the competition scenario, including:

  • Disaster relief operations in areas with limited accessibility
  • Medical supply delivery to remote locations
  • Environmental monitoring in sensitive ecosystems
  • Search and rescue mission support

The precision control systems and safety features make this platform particularly suitable for operations in populated areas or challenging terrain where conventional delivery methods may be impractical.
