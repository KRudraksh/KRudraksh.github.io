UAV Challenge 2023

  • Jan 2022 - May 2023
  • By Rudraksh Kuchiya

Developed advanced algorithms for a fully autonomous firefighting drone capable of avoiding obstacles in confined spaces. Our team was declared World Champions in the simulation stage and 2nd Runner Ups in the live trials held in Warsaw, Poland as part of the International Conference on Unmanned Aircraft Systems (ICUAS) 2023.

Project Overview

The UAV Challenge at ICUAS 2023 focused on developing autonomous drones for firefighting in industrial environments. Our team created a sophisticated drone system capable of navigating through complex, confined spaces while detecting and avoiding obstacles in real-time. The challenge required precise navigation, obstacle avoidance, and efficient path planning to reach fire sources quickly and safely.

Autonomous navigation through complex industrial environment

Technical Achievements

Advanced Navigation System

  • Developed an agile, collision-free navigation strategy for industrial environments
  • Implemented sophisticated path planning algorithms to navigate through narrow passages
  • Optimized real-time decision making for dynamic obstacle avoidance

EGO Planner Enhancements

  • Incorporated dynamic obstacle inflation to improve safety margins around obstacles
  • Implemented waypoint perturbation techniques to optimize path smoothness
  • Enhanced the existing EGO Planner framework with custom modifications for firefighting scenarios

Simulation and Testing

  • Developed comprehensive simulation environments to test navigation algorithms
  • Conducted extensive virtual testing in various industrial layouts and obstacle configurations
  • Achieved top performance in simulation trials, earning the World Champion title

Live Trial Implementation

  • Successfully translated simulation algorithms to real-world hardware
  • Adapted to physical constraints and sensor limitations in the live environment
  • Achieved 2nd Runner Up position in the competitive live trials in Warsaw

Technical Approach

Our approach focused on creating a robust autonomous system capable of operating in challenging industrial environments:

Perception System

We implemented a multi-sensor fusion approach combining LiDAR, depth cameras, and IMU data to create an accurate 3D representation of the environment. This allowed the drone to detect obstacles of various sizes and materials, even in low-visibility conditions that might be encountered during a fire.

Path Planning

Building upon the EGO Planner framework, we developed a hierarchical planning system:

  • Global planning for overall mission strategy and target identification
  • Local planning for real-time obstacle avoidance and trajectory optimization
  • Dynamic replanning capabilities to adapt to unexpected obstacles or environmental changes

Obstacle Avoidance

Our key innovation was the implementation of dynamic obstacle inflation, which adaptively adjusted safety margins based on:

  • Drone velocity and maneuverability constraints
  • Obstacle proximity and classification
  • Available free space in the environment

Waypoint Perturbation

To ensure smooth and efficient navigation, we developed a waypoint perturbation algorithm that:

  • Analyzed potential paths for smoothness and efficiency
  • Dynamically adjusted waypoints to minimize unnecessary movements
  • Optimized trajectories for energy efficiency and time-to-target

The team's innovative approach to autonomous navigation in confined spaces demonstrated exceptional performance in both simulation and real-world trials, establishing new benchmarks for UAV obstacle avoidance in industrial environments.

Impact and Applications

The technology developed for this challenge has potential applications beyond firefighting:

  • Industrial inspection of complex facilities like refineries and power plants
  • Search and rescue operations in disaster zones with unstable structures
  • Automated warehouse inventory and monitoring in tight spaces
  • Underground or confined space exploration where human access is limited or dangerous

The algorithms and navigation strategies developed during this project represent significant advancements in autonomous drone operation in challenging environments, with potential to improve safety and efficiency across multiple industries.
